About Me

From customer service to English teacher to web development. How I ended up here.

I've always loved to create things.

In high school, I was really into graphic design. I was in yearbook class for a few years, where we used photoshop, illustrator, indesign and photography to create layouts. That’s what sparked my creative side.

I studied Asian American Studies at UC Irvine, where I got my BA. As a mix between history and ethics, my major taught how to be truly empathic, which I've carried forward with me into my work and personal life.

After graduating, I didn't know what I wanted to do. I held positions in data processing, customer service, and even taught English abroad in Taiwan for two years. Although I learned a lot, these positions never felt quite stimulating enough.

Coming from a family with a tech background, I never thought this would be something that I'd develop a passion for. After self teaching then attending a 14-week full immersion web development bootcamp, I've dicovered that I enjoy waking up every day because I'll get to do what I love, which is create and solve problems. I can't wait to continue learning and improvingmy skills.

A few interesting facts about me:

  • When I'm not coding, I'm usually out hiking or doing something active.
  • I have three pet cats: Tiggy, Meowmington, and Himmy. They were all strays!
  • I love traveling and taking photos (although I'm nowhere near great at it hah).
  • I'm addicted to caffiene - gotta have my coffee and tea or I can't function!
